# Sparkz - brand and messaging

The spine, the voice, and the rules. If a surface (site, README, cast, deck) does not
sound like this, fix the surface.

## The lead line

**Start with a spark, not a token.**

This leads everywhere - site hero, README, repo description, social. Five words, whole
thesis, clean contrast. Do not bury it, do not replace it per-surface.

## The hero is the creator

The homepage speaks to **you - the artist, musician, or builder.** Second person.
Zoostr is the living proof, not a hypothetical. The community and the ecosystem are
the payoff, but the creator is who we are talking to.

## Two-tier copy

Every coined term earns its keep with a plain-English line right under it. Punchy word
on top, human sentence below. Never make a newcomer guess.

| Coined (headline) | Plain (subline) |
| --- | --- |
| Start with a spark, not a token. | Open a project's home - identity, backers, receipts. The coin comes later, if ever. |
| It's a Capsule, not a coin. | Your project's home base - everything that makes it real, in one place. |
| A spark. | A project before it's a coin. |
| The Meme Engine. | Turns a moment into a post, and a post into a permanent record. |
| Meme Receipts. | The proof - what landed, why it landed, and what it earned. |
| Boost it. | Back a creator you believe in. No wallet, no coin required. |
| Graduate. | Launch a token later, only if the momentum is real. |

## Voice

- **Punchy and Farcaster-native.** Write like a good cast, not a whitepaper. Short
  lines. Lead with the moment.
- **Creator-first, second person.** "You" and "your work," not "users."
- **No moon talk. Ever.** No pump, no 100x, no "financial advice," no ticker worship.
  The coin is plumbing, never the pitch.
- **Playful, not corporate.** The flame has personality. Have fun; stay grounded.
- **Honest.** Perks are what backers enjoy today, not promises. Shipped is shipped;
  roadmap is roadmap.

## Visual direction: playful, meme-native

Keep the bones (dark, Space Grotesk display, the flame, the violet-to-amber spark
gradient) and turn up the energy:

- **Flame-forward.** The flame is the mascot, not a bullet point. Bigger, animated,
  present. It can react (flicker, grow on hover, pulse on a boost).
- **Louder gradient.** More violet-to-amber, used with intent - CTAs, the spark word,
  the active state. Let it glow.
- **Chunkier type + sticker chips.** Heavier display weights, rounded sticker-style
  tags, playful pills. Meme-culture nods are welcome where they land.
- **Motion with purpose.** A spark that lights on load, a boost that pops the flame, a
  receipt that stamps in. Never motion for its own sake; respect reduced-motion.
- **Still legible, still dark, still mobile-first.** Playful is not messy. The
  contrast and hierarchy stay tight.

Reference feel: Farcaster-native and alive, closer to a sticker pack than a bank.

## Do / don't

- Do lead with the spark line. Don't open with the token.
- Do pair every coined term with a plain line. Don't ship jargon naked.
- Do talk to the creator. Don't talk about "users."
- Do let the flame have personality. Don't make it a static logo.
- Do keep it honest. Don't imply a feature that isn't shipped.
